Brief

 

Create a functional VR prototype aimed at teaching students relevant topics within an immersive virtual Environment. Containing interactions and utilising 3D assets.

For instance an app that teaches Film Composition Rules to students who are confused of the overwhelming Amount of information found in textbooks and videos. Are there any simpler solution.

Research

 

Users

Many University Film students have a hard time knowing where to start learning about Composition. There are so many Composition Textbooks and YouTube Videos to follow, that composition is overwhelming students, making them feel frustrated, struggling and discouraged. And reading a large body of text can be difficult to absorb information as they arenât actively learning.

Composition Cicerone VR app aims to teach University Film students (beginner and revising students), how Composition rules can be followed and broken to showcase their stories through film and this can be done through an immersive virtual environment. Having multiple different environments to explore many story possibilities. (beach, indoor, forest, ocean room and more)


User Needs

  • A character that you can bond and learn from NPC (Non Playable Character)
  • Clear Educational Message
  • Easy to learn with Visual and Audio Cues
  • Fun and simpke interaction with the app
  • How to bring VR app education to use for real world film experiences
  • Learn something new and exciting

User Testing

I approached several friends that have never studied film composition and have learnt before. They tested the prototype on their phones and they would walk me through their positive and negative experiences through tasks and interviews. I used Wizard of Oz techniques (accompany with sound) as they interact with the prototype.

Final Design

 

About Composition Cicerone

Composition Cicerone is a VR App platform that runs on Android Phones.
The VR experience would be a hand free experience and the user
would interact with it sitting down.
There will be a non-diegetic UI, containing six levels
that teach users about the following Composition Principles:

  1. Depth
  2. Leading Lines
  3. The Rule of Thirds
  4. Symmetry
  5. Break the Rules

Where players can interact with the activities through dwelling gaze. teaching them real relevant skills.

Takeaways

 

Takeaways From This Project

Composition Cicerone was a fantastic opportunity to investigate problems in an active learning space, to take a deep dive into 3D design practices and to learn how to iterate designs using a large body of users.
Challenges I have faced in this project where the coding bugs. I have learnt to try multiple computers or restarting my computer to let the game run and that would fix it. Patience and determination got all my coding to work.
I was grateful to have Henry and Callum guide me to effectively code and understand the visual designs, creating a desireable outcome, and was also grateful to advance my technical skills using Blender, Autodesk Maya, and Unity.
