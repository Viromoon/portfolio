Brief

 

The 21st century has made Australian history when carbon emissions crept down by 6.6% due to COVID 19 lockdown, solar panel installations and interstate travel restrictions. However, this is a temporary reduction as Australian modern lifestyles use lots of energy.
90% of carbon emissions in the world come from the burning of fossil fuels, and most of Australia’s emissions also come from energy production, followed by transport, agriculture, and industrial processes.

While the ocean covers about 70% of our planet. Seaweed plays a really big role in regulating our climate and if we can enhance the growth of seaweed, we can use them to develop a climate-altering crop. Seaweed saves time as they grow rapidly 60cm a day and they can sequester the carbon down to the bottom of the ocean to stay down there as a settlement.
If we could cover 9% of the world’s ocean in seaweed farms. We could draw down the equivalent of all greenhouse gases we put up in any one year and target carbon neutral by 2030.

The goal of most interaction design task is to make a complicated process feel simple and pleasant to conduct. Only showing algorithms, and show users the most important information from a large body of information.

Research

 

Users

My target audience are young climate activists who strive to make a difference in our world. I chose them specifically because the majority of that demographic, only knows about one carbon offset "Re-Planting Trees" and they are unaware of seaweed can solve carbon solutions.

FUN FACT: When searching on Google what can clean CO2 emissions it will respond with forest management, leading Young Activists to believe that is the only solution.

User Needs

  • Informed about the climate change problem and that Seaweed is the solution to the problem
  • Fun interactive informative charts
  • How users can make a call to action (Donate or help out)

Problem

How might we…

Create…an enticing , interactive, informative website
For…Young Climate Activists
To…make young audiences know that Seaweed is the best Carbon Offset to use to reach net zero carbon
And Improve… the quality of life. By having more seaweed farms we can use it for many benefits, replacing plastic bags, textiles, cow emissions and more.

Takeaways

 

Takeaways From This Project

Superhero Seaweed was a fantastic opportunity to be more knowledgeable about Climate Change and Seaweed as a Carbon Offset. Learning how huge amounts of information can be made smaller and simplistic. Make Plotly JS accessible to any device size and to learn how to iterate designs using my users feedbacks.
Challenges I have faced is reading all of the data sources and make it simple for the user. The way I would solve this is to understand what the user needs and only keep the information that makes sense to them.
I was grateful to have Ana guide me to effectively code and understand colour theory, and was also grateful to advance my technical skills using Visual Studio Code, Illustrator and Excel Sheets.
